Stage 5 Elective 2027

What could you study in Geography?

Geography at Stage 5 is about the real world — conflict, climate, food, oceans, power and people. Every unit connects to the UN Sustainable Development Goals and leads directly into Stage 6.

Unit 1
Extreme Environments
Could you survive in the Sahara? Why do people choose to live where the Earth is trying to kill them?
What you study
Deserts, polar regions, high-altitude zones and wetlands — how physical processes create harsh conditions and how humans and ecosystems adapt to survive.
Why it matters
Climate change is pushing more of the world into extreme conditions. Understanding these environments is critical to managing future risk for billions of people.
Careers
Climate scientistEmergency managementEnvironmental engineerPark rangerAdventure tourism
Links to the SDGs
SDG 13 — Climate Action SDG 15 — Life on Land SDG 6 — Clean Water

Unit 2
Geopolitics
Why do countries go to war over lines on a map? Who really controls the world's resources?
What you study
Power, territory and conflict — how borders are drawn and contested, how nations compete for resources, and how geopolitical decisions shape the daily lives of ordinary people.
Why it matters
From Ukraine to the South China Sea, geopolitics is live news. Understanding it helps you make sense of the world rather than just watch it unfold.
Careers
Diplomat / foreign affairsIntelligence analystJournalistInternational lawNGO work

Unit 3
Global Citizenship
What does it mean to be responsible for something you've never seen? Can one person's choices change the world?
What you study
Global connections, shared responsibilities and local action — sustainability, ethical consumption, human rights and what it means to belong to a world community.
Why it matters
The choices you make every day — what you buy, eat, and post — are connected to people and ecosystems on the other side of the planet.

Development workerSustainability consultantSocial entrepreneurHuman rights lawEducation

Unit 4
Oceanography
The ocean covers 71% of Earth — and we've explored less than 20% of it. What's down there?
What you study
Ocean systems, currents, marine ecosystems, sea-level rise, coral bleaching and the growing crisis of ocean pollution — including microplastics and ocean acidification.
Why it matters
Australia has one of the world's longest coastlines. The ocean regulates our climate, feeds billions, and is under unprecedented pressure from human activity.

Marine biologistOceanographerCoastal engineerEnvironmental policyBlue economy

Unit 5
Australia's Neighbours
Why do events in Indonesia, Timor-Leste and the Pacific Islands matter so much to Australia's future?
What you study
The physical and human geography of our region — population, development, environmental challenges, cultural diversity and Australia's interconnections with the Indo-Pacific.
Why it matters
Australia's security, economy and cultural identity are deeply tied to the region. Understanding our neighbours is not optional — it is essential.

Aid and developmentDefence / foreign policyTrade and commerceCultural liaisonTourism

Unit 6
Primary Production
How do we feed 8 billion people — and what happens when the land, water or climate can no longer keep up?
What you study
Agriculture, aquaculture, forestry and mining — how primary industries use and transform environments, the pressures of global food demand, and the future of sustainable production.
Why it matters
Australia is one of the world's leading food exporters. From drought to supply chains to genetically modified crops, these decisions shape what ends up on your plate.
