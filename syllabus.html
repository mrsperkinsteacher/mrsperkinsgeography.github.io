Stage 5 Geography Elective
Years 9–10 · Starting 2027
This elective gives you the chance to go deeper into Geography than the mandatory course allows. You'll investigate real environments, real conflicts and real people - and develop the skills to think and write like a geographer. It's challenging, relevant and genuinely interesting.
What you'll study
Extreme Environments
Geopolitics
Global Citizenship
Oceanography
Australia's Neighbours
Primary Production
What's expected of you
  • Engage seriously with each unit - read, question, and bring your own examples to class discussions
  • Develop geographical skills: map reading, data interpretation, fieldwork observation and spatial analysis
  • Write responses that describe, explain and evaluate - not just list facts
  • Connect each unit to the UN Sustainable Development Goals and real-world case studies
  • Complete all assessment tasks on time and to the best of your ability
  • Think independently - the best geographers bring ideas nobody else has thought of
How you'll be assessed
Task type
Geographical Inquiry
Research and written response investigating a real geographical issue
Task type
Skills Assessment
Map work, data analysis and spatial skills tasks throughout the course
Task type
Examination
Written exam testing knowledge, understanding and geographical thinking
Task type
Field Investigation
Observation and data collection in a real environment outside the classroom

Stage 6 Preliminary Geography
Year 11 · 2022 Syllabus
Preliminary Geography builds the conceptual and skills foundation for your HSC year. You'll study three focus areas - exploring how natural systems work, how people shape and are shaped by places, and what drives human wellbeing around the world. This year is about depth of thinking, not breadth of content.
What you'll study
Natural Systems
People, Patterns and Processes
Human–Environment Interactions
Geographical Investigation
What's expected of you
  • Develop sophisticated geographical skills - including spatial technologies, fieldwork and data analysis
  • Build extended response writing skills that explain processes, analyse patterns and evaluate strategies
  • Use specific, well-chosen case studies to support every argument you make
  • Engage with both physical and human geography - the Preliminary course covers both
  • Manage your time and study independently - Year 11 requires self-discipline as well as ability
  • Begin thinking about your Depth Study topic for HSC - what do you genuinely want to investigate?
How you'll be assessed
Task type
Fieldwork Investigation
Practical data collection and analysis from a real field study site
Task type
Research Task
In-depth investigation of a geographical issue using multiple sources
Task type
Examinations
Half-yearly and yearly exams testing all three Preliminary topics
Task type
Spatial Technologies
GIS and mapping tasks showing your ability to interpret spatial data

Stage 6 HSC Geography
Year 12 · 2022 Syllabus
HSC Geography is where everything comes together. You'll study three focus areas including an individual depth study - a genuine geographical investigation into something you care about. The HSC exam is 3 hours and rewards students who think clearly, write precisely and use evidence well.
What you'll study
Global Sustainability
Rural and Urban Places
Ecosystems and Global Biodiversity
Depth Study - your own inquiry
What's expected of you
  • Master extended response writing - HSC Geography rewards sustained, structured analytical writing
  • Know your case studies inside out - specific details, locations, dates and outcomes
  • Complete your Depth Study with genuine intellectual rigour - this is your chance to show what you can do independently
  • Practice past HSC questions regularly - the exam format rewards familiarity and technique
  • Be able to evaluate - not just describe or explain, but judge the effectiveness and significance of geographical processes and strategies
  • Show up for every lesson - Year 12 Geography moves fast and every class builds on the last
How you'll be assessed
School-based
Depth Study
Individual geographical inquiry - your own question, your own research, your own conclusions
School-based
Trial HSC Exam
Full 3-hour exam under HSC conditions - essential preparation for October
External
HSC Examination
3-hour written exam in October - Section I (short answer) and Section II (extended responses)
Weighting
50% / 50%
School assessment 50% + HSC exam 50% = your final HSC mark
Exam structure at a glance
  • Section I - 25 marks: short-answer questions on core topics and stimulus material
  • Section II - 25 marks: two extended responses (one on each core topic)
  • Section III - 25 marks: Depth Study extended response
  • Total - 75 marks in 3 hours: time management is a genuine skill to practise
