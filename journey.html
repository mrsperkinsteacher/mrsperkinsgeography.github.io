Your Geography Journey

Geography is for curious people. If you've ever wondered why the world is the way it is - you're already a geographer.

You belong here.
Geography isn't just a subject - it's a way of seeing the world. It asks the questions that matter: Why do people live where they do? Who has power and who doesn't? What happens to a place when the climate changes? How do ordinary decisions - yours included - shape the planet?

You don't need to be an expert. You need to be curious. There are no wrong answers in Geography. There are only deeper questions.
Where you are now
Stage 5
Years 9–10
You're building the foundations - how to read the world, use evidence, and form a geographical argument. This is where curiosity becomes a skill. Every unit connects to something happening right now in the real world.
Elective
Choosing Geography
Choosing Geography for Stage 5 means choosing to engage with the real world. Six units, six different lenses on how the planet works - from extreme environments to geopolitics to the oceans.
Prelim
Year 11
Preliminary Geography builds your toolkit. You'll study biophysical interactions, changing places and human wellbeing - learning to think spatially, use data, and construct sophisticated geographical arguments.
HSC
Year 12
HSC Geography goes deep. Ecosystems at risk, global integration and a depth study where you investigate something you genuinely care about - your own geographical inquiry, your own voice.
How to learn well in Geography
️
Connect everything to a place
Geography lives in the real world. Whenever you learn a concept, ask: where does this happen? Find it on a map. Give it a name. Make it real. Abstract ideas become powerful when they're anchored to an actual place.
Read the news differently
Every news story is a Geography story. A flood, an election, a food shortage, a border dispute - start asking why it's happening there, and what geographical factors came before it. This is how geographers think.
️
Write to think, not just to answer
The best Geography responses don't just describe - they explain, analyse and evaluate. Practice adding "this matters because..." after every point you make. That one habit will transform your writing.
Use data to back yourself up
A well-chosen statistic makes your argument impossible to ignore. Our World in Data has a number for everything - use it. Specific figures show markers you know your content, not just your opinion.
Ask the uncomfortable questions
Who benefits? Who is left out? Whose story isn't being told? Geography is at its best when it challenges you to think beyond the obvious answer. The most interesting responses always go a layer deeper.

Want to go further?
Go deeper
Read beyond the textbook
Try Prisoners of Geography by Tim Marshall - it explains world politics through maps and physical geography. Or The Uninhabitable Earth by David Wallace-Wells for a confronting look at climate futures. Start with one chapter.
Show more
Use data in your responses
Our World in Data has a statistic for everything. A well-chosen number makes your argument impossible to ignore - and it shows markers you've gone beyond the basics.
Push harder
Find a case study that surprises
Don't use the same examples as everyone else. Find a place, a story, a situation that nobody else in your class will use. Surprising case studies stay in a marker's mind.
Do more
Take Geography outside the classroom
Notice your local area. How has it changed? Who lives there? What does the built environment tell you about power, money and planning decisions? Your street is a Geography resource.
